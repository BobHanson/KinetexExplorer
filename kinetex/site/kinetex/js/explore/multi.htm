What happens when there are two steps in a reaction?
Look carefully at this list, which summarizes the changes
in concentration of the various species in solution.
Notice that after a brief initial increase in [I],
the [I] becomes very low and steadily decreases.
Because of the presence of the intermediate in solution,
the rule "rate=-d[A]/dt=-d[B]/dt=d[C]/dt" is not quite true.
Still, after that initial phase, it's a pretty good approximation.
We call it the steady-state approximation.
See what happens when you switch the values of k1 and k2.
Our model only works when the first irreversible step is the slow one.
